The body of the athlete is in a shape of a lever, it means two straight unbent lines crossing at a pivot point.

Line 1 is the line joining the hand holding to the bar and the shoulders of the athlete: the alignment of the different joints is maintained by the triceps and the forearm muscles; straightening the arm is quiet an easy task.

Line 2 is the line going from the shoulders to the very end of the toe’s phalanges. The alignment of the joints is maintained by a huge amount of muscles working together to fulfill the task. All the vertebras have to be placed at a specific position to align the back. The pelvis has to be placed and rotated to a specific degree to hold the legs is a horizontal position. The inferior limbs must stay in a straight line to balance the body. Foot position is not very important due to the fact that an average foot weights 400 grams.

The alignment if the vertebras and the rotation of the pelvis are set by the CORE, the muscle group that joins the thoracic cage and the pelvis and that controls the body position in almost every skill in the sport. Having a string core is a must in the front lever.

The pulling strength you get from calisthenics is insane, the force you can generate from your back and arms muscles is incredible and can exceed 100 kg very easily.

The trick with the front lever is the position of the elbow, it must be in a straight unbent position wish requires a good triceps strength on order to execute the extension of the joint. This position puts more stress on the pulling muscles in the back as all the weight is now lifted by the extensors of the superior limbs.

Straight arms strength is an important component of many skills in calisthenics like the planche and the front lever. This strength can be improved overtime through straight arms exercise. If you unlock this component, you can easily jump to the next level with bent arms front lever skills like the touch front lever or front lever pullups.

Motion is a human body feature that combines different components to move a body part.

It all starts with the decision making in the respective cortex of the brain, then it travels through the motor cortex (4 & 6) to go through a specific nerve for each muscle. The process of making the decision to execute a skill and to transfer it to the muscle is very complex, and it can be improved in many ways in order to improve the execution of the skill.

The decision of doing the skill in itself is complex, and it needs a pre-elaborated pattern of muscles recruitment to position the body in the horizontal position. This pattern is improved overtime through repetition and consistent effort and will to improve.

The transfer of the information is improved overtime through the reconstruction of synaptic connections to transfer the message very quickly and effetely without information loss.

The contraction of the muscle is improved with various strengthening exercises.

This process helps overtime, build a string muscle memory that is hard to lose even after years of no practice. You never forget how to ride a bike.

REQUIREMENTS

Discipline
A consistent number of pullups (~10): pull strength.
A consistent number of dips: straight arms strength.
